Breathe Easy with BreatheWell

Air pollution refers to the contamination of the air by harmful substances. These substances can be solid particles, like dust and smoke, or gaseous pollutants like nitrogen dioxide and ozone. BreatheWell is your one-stop shop for understanding air quality in Southeast Asia. We leverage real-time data and interactive maps to empower you to make informed decisions about your health.

To monitor these harmful effects, government agencies and environmental organizations around the world use an Air Quality Index (AQI) to track pollution levels. The AQI is a numerical value that reflects the concentration of various pollutants in the air, along with a corresponding color-coded scale that allows for easy interpretation.

A low AQI indicates good air quality, while a high AQI signifies unhealthy air conditions. These AQIs are calculated using data collected from air quality monitoring stations located throughout a city or region. By tracking air quality through AQIs and APIs, we can gain valuable insights into the health of our environment and take action to reduce air pollution and its negative impacts.

Air Quality Index Scaling Guide

AQI Category Description Health Concerns Actions
0-50 Good Minimal impact Enjoy your outdoor activities!
51-100 Moderate May cause minor breathing discomfort for some people Continue usual activities.
101-150 Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups May cause moderate breathing problems for people with asthma, heart disease, or lung disease Reduce outdoor activity, especially for children and older adults.
151-200 Unhealthy Everyone may begin to experience health effects; for some, it may cause more serious breathing problems Reduce outdoor activity. Consider using air purifiers indoors.
201-300 Very Unhealthy Health warnings of serious effects. Avoid outdoor activity. Everyone may experience more serious health effects.
301-500 Hazardous Health alert: everyone may experience serious health effects. Stay indoors and avoid all outdoor exertion.
